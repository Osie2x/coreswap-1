PRODUCT DESCRIPTION

EcoFill is a loose-fill and dense-pack cellulose insulation manufactured from
a minimum of 85% post-consumer recycled newsprint and other recovered paper
fibres. The remaining constituents are non-halogenated fire retardants
(ammonium sulphate and boric acid compounds, < 15% by mass). No formaldehyde,
bleach, or synthetic binders are used in production.

Recycled content: 85–90% post-consumer, 5–10% pre-consumer.

This EPD represents an industry-average dataset for cellulose insulation
manufactured by CIMA member companies in Canada, based on 2022 production data.

DECLARED UNIT

1 square metre of installed cellulose insulation at a nominal thermal resistance
of RSI 3.52 (R-20 imperial) at typical dry-installed density of 48 kg/m³
for dense-pack wall application, with a nominal installed thickness of 140 mm.

SYSTEM BOUNDARIES: A1–A3 (Cradle to Gate)

Biogenic Carbon Treatment: This EPD applies the approach of EN 15804+A2
Annex B for biogenic carbon accounting. Carbon sequestered in the recycled
paper fibre feedstock is included in A1 as a negative (storage) contribution.
Re-release of stored biogenic carbon at end of life is outside the system
boundary of this A1-A3 EPD.

The A1-A3 total GWP is -7.50 kg CO2e per square metre at RSI 3.52 (R-20).

The dominant term is GWP-biogenic of -9.10 kg CO2e/m², reflecting the temporary
biogenic carbon storage in the recycled paper feedstock. The fossil GWP of
+1.60 kg CO2e/m² reflects energy use in shredding, treatment, and transport.
The net total GWP is therefore negative: this product is a net carbon sink
on a cradle-to-gate basis.

This result is consistent with the CIMA BEAM (Building Environmental Assessment
Methodology) dataset: mid-point industry estimate -7.85 kg CO2e/m².
